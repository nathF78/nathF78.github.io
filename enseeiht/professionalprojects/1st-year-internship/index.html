1st Year Summer Job

Nathan | Dec 6, 2022

Aknowlegments

First of all, I would like to thank my boss, Mr. Dubois Marc, for giving me the opportunity to work in his company. I would also like to thank my colleagues for their help and their kindness.

Also I would like to thank the clients for their understanding and their patience during my first days.

Introduction

Compagnie Presentation

Marché Frais Orgeval is a company that sells fresh products. It is located in Orgeval, a small town in the Yvelines department. The company is composed of 3 stores and 2 warehouses owned by Mr. Dubois Marc, who is also the director of the store.

Marché Frais sells fresh products such as fruits, vegetables, meat, fish, cheese, etc. The store also sells frozen products such as ice cream, frozen vegetables, etc. It sells exclusivly good quality products (with often high prices) and is known for it.

The team I’ve joined

I worked as a cashier in the store. I was working with 6 other cashiers. One of them, Véronique, was my formator. She was very kind and she helped me a lot during my first days. The majority of team was composed with young people, between 18 and 25 years old. Some of them were students, others were working in the company for a long time. I had to chance to make friends with them really quickly which enabled me to be more comfortable in the beginning.

The job

My responsabilities

My main responsabilities were to cash the clients and help them if they needed it. I also had to clean my register and sometimes a part of the store or help the other cashiers if they needed it.

My biggest responsability was to deal with the money. Thankfully I didn’t need to count it at the end of the day because the registers do it automatically. Nethertless, I had to make sure that clients payed the right price to avoid any problem with the them or with the store. At the end of the day I had to close the cash register and make sure that the money account was correct. If something was missing it was my own responsability.

What I’ve done

I worked 4 days a week with a 35 hours contract. I mostly worked from 9 am to 8 pm with an 1h break during lunchtime and 15m in the afternoon.

At the beginning there was an error on my contract and I worked in the warehouse instead of the store. I was really disappointed because I wanted to work inside the store and talk to the clients. In the warehouse I was working with one other person my age and we had to transport heavy boxes from one place to another. It was really hard and I was really tired at the end of the day. Finally, after talking to my manager the problem has been solved the next week which made me really happy.

So, when I’ve finally became a cashier I first had a week of formation. In the store, most of the products didn’t have a price tag (since it’s mostly fruits and vegetables) so I had to learn every product code. There was around 200 codes to learn so I’ve made a little booklet with all of them classed by products names. In fact I’ve managed to learn them quite quickly.

Sometimes instead of cashing clients I had other mission. On of them was to help my in-store colleagues to replace and make the inventory. But more amusing, since my collegues knew I was in a Computer Sciences Engineer school, I’ve quiclky became the IT guy. I was often called to help with bugged registres. Another fact, I’ve also became the official english traductor of the store and was called to help clients who didn’t speak french (which was quite often, mostly with americans).

What I’ve learned

With this job I learned a lot of things. First of all, I learned how to deal with money. I learned how to count it and how to make sure that the money account was correct. I also learned how to deal with clients, how to be patient and how to help them if they needed it. I also learned how to work in a team, how to work with other people and how to help them if they needed it. Finally, I learned how to deal with responsabilities and hyeraarchie.

This job also oppened my eyes to the world of hard work. In fact I never worked in such a job. Before I only did internship linked with IT and I never had to work with my hands. Also, talking to my collegues, it enabled me to see that some people work in a job they don’t like because they have to pay their bills which was quite uncommon for me because all my family work in a high qualified job they like (mostly as engineers or teachers).

Conclusion

In fact, I didn’t really liked this job because it was mostly anoying and not intellectually challenging, I was just a “robot”. But thanks to it, my vision of work and life clearly changed. I’m so grateful and happy to become an engineer because I know that I will never have to work in a job I don’t like since I’m passionate about Computer Sciences.

Netherless this jobs thaught me a lot of things that, I’m convinced, will be usefull in my future life thanks to my awesome and kinds collegues.
